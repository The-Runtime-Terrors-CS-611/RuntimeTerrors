Julia Finn Scenario #2

Using the Game to Teach Narrative Story Arcs

Julia Finn is a 5th grade teacher in Aurora, Colorado. Today, she is teaching her students about narrative writing and wants to introduce narrative story arcs. She recently discovered a choose your own adventure game called Forged in Fire, and wants to try to use this game as an engaging way to talk about the progression of narratives.

Julia printed out some worksheets of narrative story arcs with blank spaces for the children to fill in for each part of the arc- exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, and resolution. The students will play the game, making choices as they go, and fill in the worksheet based on the story that they help create. Julia assigns them the game and worksheet as homework. Once they have completed their homework, she assigns them to random groups of three, and instructs them to share their work.

While walking around the room, she realizes that many of the story arcs are almost exactly the same. This defeats the purpose of her students sharing different examples of narrative story arcs.
