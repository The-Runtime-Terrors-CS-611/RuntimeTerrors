Ben Interview #2

Interview Info

  • Date: 1/28/2026
  • Interviewer: Ben Waddell
  • Duration: 45 Minutes

Top Takeaways

  • Strong use case is deployed / on ship: they want something that works offline and fits short sessions.
  • They expect meaningful consequences where choices clearly lock you out of options, change outcomes, or create tradeoffs (hurt/slow you down, can’t do X anymore).
  • They want more than a “maze to 2–3 endings”; choices should feel important and produce real variety.
  • They haven’t played CYOA before, but the concept appeals because it feels unique and they like the thrill of the unknown and choices affecting outcomes.
  • Aesthetically, they’re open to art and mentioned a preference for pixelated style if you include visuals.

Top Concerns/Turn-offs

  • Bad grammar / writing quality is an immediate turn-off.
  • Instant death early on, and feeling railroaded by the story or “fake choices.”
  • They don’t want current-issues / political commentary baked into the story.

Top Desired Features

  • Save/Load (especially important for mobile + interruptions and deployment)
  • Downloadable/offline support (explicitly needed to play on the ship)
  • More storylines / branching variety (avoid only 2–3 endings)

Notable Quote (Optional)

“A lot of those stories… are just like a maze to end up at one of [two or three endings]. That makes the choices feel unimportant.”
